What is GIS

GIS is fundamentally the study of Geographic Science and how we can analyse and understand the information that we collect. The Information that we collect can be used to answer questions about the world. Spatial data can be used to answer a wide variety of topics- From the migration patterns of swallows to finding how close you are to an ice cream store, it has a wide variety of uses. We can use GIS to find the relation between places and patterns of distribution

GIS programs allow us to store, organise and visualise data using maps. The use of layers allow us to combine, hide and compare information. We can show rivers and roads and many other forms of information according to the needs of the Task.

GIS programs allow for the use of raster analysis to solve real life problems. As we see in this course, the information that we gain can be used for a wide variety of tasks, from flood analysis to vegetation monitoring through satellite remote sensing. Gis can help us make better decisions about complex problems that we face in veryday life, helping us make better cities, countries and a better world
